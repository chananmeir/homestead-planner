**Column notes:**
- **Days to Maturity**: Days from planting/transplanting to harvest. Trees show days to first fruit (years).
- **Start Indoors (wks)**: How many weeks to grow indoors before transplanting. `-` means direct sow, not applicable, or planted as nursery stock.
- **Outdoor Planting (wks from last frost)**: Positive = weeks before last frost. Negative = weeks after last frost.
- **Method**: DS = direct sow (seed goes straight in the ground). TP = transplant (started indoors from seed, or planted as nursery stock/bare-root for trees).
- **Frost Tolerance**: very-hardy > hardy > half-hardy > tender > very-tender
- **Ideal Seasons**: `-` means not specified in the database.

**Data notes:**
- **Lavender** frost tolerance is listed as `hardy` but is variety-dependent. English lavender (*Lavandula angustifolia*) is hardy to Zone 5, while French/Spanish varieties are tender (Zone 8+).
- **Rice** is included for completeness but is an experimental/specialty crop for most homesteaders. Upland rice varieties can be grown in non-flooded conditions.
- **Fruit trees & berry bushes** are marked TP (transplant) because they are planted as nursery stock, not grown from seed indoors. Their "Start Indoors" column shows `-` accordingly.
